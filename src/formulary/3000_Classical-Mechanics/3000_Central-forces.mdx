<Equation

<Equation
  label="Reduced mass"
  latex="\mu \equiv \dfrac{m_1 m_2}{m_1 + m_2}"
  notes
/>

* Equivalently, $\mu^{-1} = m_1^{-1} + m_2^{-1}$.
* The results on this page are for the equivalent one-body problem (as shown in Fig. 1) of the central force motion of two bodies $m_1$ and $m_2$ about their center of mass. Thus $m$ is replaced by $\mu$.

## Gravitational (inverse-square) force

<Equation
  label="Gravitational potential"
  latex="V(r) = - \dfrac{\gamma \mu}{r}"
/>

<Equation
  label=""
  latex="\gamma \equiv G(m_1 + m_2)"
/>

<Equation
  label="Solution of orbital EoM"
  latex="\dfrac{1}{r} = C(1-\varepsilon \cos{\varphi})"
  notes={true}
  important={true}
  reference="orbital-eom-solution"
/>

* Solution of <EquationRef reference="orbital-eom" />.
* This is the equation of a conic section with one focus at the origin, where $\varphi_0=0$ by convention.
* Keep in mind that here $E$ and $l$ are the energy and angular momentum in the center-of-mass frame.

<Equation
  label=""
  latex="C \equiv \dfrac{\gamma \mu^2}{l^2}"
/>

<Equation
  label="Eccentricity"
  latex="\varepsilon = \left(1 + \dfrac{2El^2}{\mu^3 \gamma^2} \right)^{1/2}"
/>

Eccentricity | Energy | Orbit
:---|:---|:---
$\varepsilon>1$ | $E>0$ | Hyperbola
$\varepsilon=1$ | $E=0$ | Parabola
$0<\varepsilon<1$ | $V_{\text{min}}<E<0$ | Ellipse
$\varepsilon=0$ | $E=V_{\text{min}}$ | Circle

### Kepler's laws

<Equation
  label="1st Law"
  latex="r^{-1}=C(1-\varepsilon \cos{\varphi})"
  notes={true}
/>

*Planets move in elliptical orbits about the Sun with the Sun at one focus.*

<Equation
  label="2nd Law"
  latex="\dfrac{dA}{dt} = \dfrac{l}{2\mu} = \text{constant}"
  notes={true}
/>

*The area per unit time swept out by a radius vector from the Sun to a planet is constant.*

<Equation
  label="3rd Law"
  latex="\tau^2 = \dfrac{4 \pi^2 a^3}{\gamma} \cong \dfrac{4 \pi^2 a^3}{Gm_{\text{Sun}}}"
  notes={true}
/>

*The square of a planet's period is proportional to the cube of the major axis of the planet's orbit*

## Scattering

Unbounded, hyperbolic orbits ($E > 0$) in a gravitational potential.

<Figures
  filenames={[

<Equation
  label="Energy"
  latex="E = \tfrac{1}{2} \mu v_0^2"
  notes={true}
/>

* Energy in the center-of-mass frame.
* $v_0$ is the relative speed of particles approaching from infinity.

<Equation
  label="Angular momentum"
  latex="l = \mu v_0 b"
  notes={true}
/>

* Angular momentum in the center-of-mass frame.
* $b$ is called the impact parameter and is the distance of closest approach if the trajectory were undeflected.

<Equation
  label="Eccentricity"
  latex="\varepsilon = \bigg[1 + \bigg(\dfrac{v_0^2 b}{\gamma}\bigg)^2 \bigg]^{1/2}"
/>

<Equation
  label="Distance of closest approach"
  latex="r_{\text{min}} = b \left( \dfrac{\varepsilon - 1}{\varepsilon + 1} \right)^{1/2}"
/>

<Equation
  label="Deflection angle"
  latex="\theta = 2 \arctan{\dfrac{\gamma}{v_0^2 b}}"
/>
